Celebrating Tradition and Community in the 67th Parada ng Lechon in Balayan, Batangas

Balayan, Batangas

Author: Nyl   Category: South Luzon   Date: May 2026

RCD Land, honored to serve as one of the Gold Sponsors of this event, joined the 67th Parada ng Lechon sa Balayan last June 24 with a beautifully and traditionally designed float that reflected the spirit of Balayan’s culture and the festive identity of the parade.

The float stood as a tribute not only to the town’s artistic expression but also to the enduring values of unity, faith, and celebration that define the community.

Beyond the parade, the day was made even more meaningful as the RCD Land team and sellers gathered for a shared meal at RCD Royale Homes Balayan, Batangas. It was a simple yet heartfelt moment of togetherness—strengthening bonds, sharing stories, and celebrating success as one community.

IN PHOTO: RCD Land Sellers enjoy a shared lunch at the RCD Royale Homes Balayan Site Office after participating in the Parada ng Lechon, taking a well-deserved break following the celebration.

For RCD Land, participation in events like the Parada ng Lechon goes beyond building homes. It is about being part of something bigger—preserving tradition, honoring culture, and helping shape meaningful lives.

Through community celebrations, RCD Land continues to affirm its commitment to uplifting not just families, but the spirit of the communities they serve.

As the festivities come to a close, RCD Land extends its deepest gratitude to the people of Balayan, its partners, sellers, and everyone who supported and celebrated with them.

IN PHOTO: RCD Land Sellers together with the RCD Land Sales and Marketing team take a group selfie as the parade comes to an end, capturing a moment of unity, celebration, and shared success

The warmth of the community made the event truly unforgettable, and RCD Land looks forward to continuing its journey of building both homes and shared traditions for years to come.

At its core, the Parada ng Lechon in Balayan, Batangas is a vibrant cultural festival held in honor of St. John the Baptist. Known for its lively street parade and the symbolic “baptism” of lechon, it highlights the town’s deep-rooted faith, creativity, and strong sense of community.
